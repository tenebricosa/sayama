Thailand

CULTURE

Thais venerate their old traditions and customs. In Thailand, show the same degree of respect that you would in your native country. Please make sure you behave and dress modestly. Being shirtless or wearing shorts, mini-skirts and other provocative outfits is not permitted in cultural heritage sites. When visiting a Buddhist sanctuary, you will be required to wear appropriate clothing, which means all knees and shoulders must be covered.

You may keep your shoes on only outside the temple. When stepping over the threshold of a temple, make sure you take them off. All statues and images of Buddha – small, large, crumbling or new – are regarded as sacred. Please don’t climb on them, even to pose for a photo. Treat them with respect.

Don’t get too emotional in public and don’t raise your voice.

HM the King and the Royal Family are honored an deeply revered by Thais. Any sign of disregard for him including his image on the local banknotes are seen as a crime.

SAFETY

While the tap water found throughout Thailand is undrinkable, hotels provide complimentary safe drinking water. You can also buy bottled drinking water in all shops. Thailand is a left-hand drive country and traffic here is busy and sometimes chaotic. Please be very careful when crossing the road.

Please refrain from renting motocycles and scooters – you may fall a victim of a fraud or scam involving documents or deposit, or get into a car accident.

Please make sure you keep all your valuables in a safe in your hotel room or at the reception. It is advisable that you make photocopies of your air tickets, passport and insurance policy and keep them separately or have them about yourself.

MONEY

In Thailand, only local currency, the baht, is accepted. If you buy something you may pay in cash or by a credit card. Money can be exchanged in banks or in official exchange offices.

VAT REFUND

When buying goods valued at more than 2,000 THB, you should ask a sales assistance for a special VAT refund form. You will also need to show her your passport to allow her to fill in the above mentioned form, so that you can easily get a 7% VAT refund at the airport before departure.

HOW TO RENT/BUY REAL ESTATE?

For all questions relating to acquisition of real estate in Thailand you should contact duly licensed and reputable companies only. Such companies provide reliable legal advice and support at all stages.
